## Design Context

### Users
College students and young professionals who want to passively reflect on their day. They open the app at night, before bed — a quiet, personal moment. Zero input required; the app records location and time automatically throughout the day. The job to be done: "show me how I spent my day in a way that makes me want to look."

### Brand Personality
**Intimate, refined, unhurried.**

Attune (working name: Unfold) is not a productivity tool, not a health tracker, not a social app. It's a personal artifact — like a beautifully bound journal you never had to write in. It observes without judging, presents without lecturing.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Quiet luxury meets Apple-level restraint. Warm but not cozy. Minimal but not sterile. Every pixel should feel considered.

**References:**
- **Flighty** — Data-dense yet elegant, deep color work, aviation-inspired confidence. Aspire to this level of craft in data presentation.
- **Arc Browser** — Bold color choices, personality without being childish, breaks convention while staying polished.
- **Apple Weather** — Dynamic, layered information hierarchy, atmospheric.
- **Apple Journal** — Warm texture, card-based, intimate.

**Anti-references (never look like):**
- Health/fitness apps — No dashboards, progress rings, charts, or gamification. Attune presents feelings, not metrics.
- Cute/cartoon style — No oversized rounded corners, colorful emoji, or infantile aesthetics. This is an adult product.
- Quantified-self tools — No scores, streaks, or "you walked 8,234 steps today."

**Theme:** Both light and dark mode. Light mode uses warm cream (#F7F5F0) backgrounds with warm-tinted neutrals — never pure gray or pure white. Dark mode uses warm dark tones, not cold blue-black.

**Color system (already established):**
- Accent: Teal (#5B9A8B light / #7CC1AF dark)
- Background: Warm cream (#F8F5F0) / Warm dark (#121213)
- Surface: Warm white (#FFFDFA) / (#1C1C1E)
- Semantic colors: Sleep (indigo), Workout (amber), Walk (green), Mood (rose), Screen (purple), Commute (blue), Echo (teal)
- Shadows: Always warm-tinted (brown-based), never neutral gray
- Rule: No pure black, no pure gray — always tint toward warmth

### Design Principles

1. **Reduction is the feature.** Strip every element to its essence. If removing something doesn't hurt comprehension, remove it. White space is a design element, not empty space.

2. **Passive beauty.** The interface should be beautiful even when the user isn't interacting. The timeline is art, not a data table. Design for the moment someone opens it at 11pm and just... looks.

3. **Native fluency.** Feel like an Apple first-party app. iOS spring animations, SF Symbols, system blur materials where appropriate. Motion should be fluid and natural — never bouncy/elastic, never jarring.

4. **Warm precision.** Every color value is exact (hex, not descriptions). Every spacing follows the 4pt grid. Warmth comes from carefully chosen tints, not from decoration.

5. **Information through atmosphere.** Communicate time-of-day through color gradients (sunrise gold → noon warmth → sunset amber → night indigo), not through numbers. The user should *feel* when something happened, not read when it happened.

### Typography
- System fonts (SF Pro) for native feel
- Bold titles, regular body, caption for metadata
- Monospaced for timestamps only
- Hierarchy through weight and size, not color variation

### Motion
- iOS native spring animations (`animation(.spring)`)
- Smooth transitions between states
- Subtle parallax on scroll where appropriate
- Purpose-driven: every animation communicates state change or spatial relationship
- No bounce, no elastic, no playful — fluid and considered
